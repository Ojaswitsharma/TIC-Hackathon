1. CODE OF CONDUCT

1.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct. This includes treating colleagues, customers, and partners with respect and dignity. Harassment, discrimination, or unprofessional behavior will not be tolerated.

1.2 Conflict of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. Any potential conflicts must be disclosed to management immediately. Outside employment or business activities that compete with company interests are prohibited.

1.3 Confidentiality
Employees must protect confidential information including customer data, business strategies, financial information, and proprietary technology. Confidential information may not be shared with unauthorized parties or used for personal gain.

2. EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

2.1 Equal Opportunity Employment
We are an equal opportunity employer committed to workplace diversity. Employment decisions are based on merit, qualifications, and business needs without regard to race, gender, religion, age, disability, or other protected characteristics.

2.2 Workplace Safety
All employees are responsible for maintaining a safe work environment. Safety incidents must be reported immediately. Personal protective equipment must be used as required. Workplace violence or threats will result in immediate termination.

2.3 Drug and Alcohol Policy
The use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs or alcohol on company premises is strictly prohibited. Employees reporting to work under the influence will face disciplinary action up to and including termination.

3. INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY POLICIES

3.1 Acceptable Use
Company IT resources are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted but must not interfere with work duties. Prohibited activities include accessing inappropriate content, downloading unauthorized software, or using company resources for commercial purposes.

3.2 Data Protection
All employees must follow data protection protocols. This includes using strong passwords, encrypting sensitive data, and reporting security incidents immediately. Personal devices used for work must comply with security standards.

3.3 Email and Communication
Email and other communication systems are company property and may be monitored. Professional communication standards must be maintained. Spam, chain letters, or inappropriate content distribution is prohibited.

4. SAFETY AND SECURITY POLICIES

4.1 Physical Security
Employees must wear ID badges at all times while on company premises. Visitors must be escorted and sign in at reception. Doors to secure areas must be kept locked. Suspicious activity should be reported to security immediately.

4.2 Emergency Procedures
Emergency evacuation routes are posted throughout the building. Fire drills are conducted quarterly. In case of emergency, follow evacuation procedures and report to designated assembly areas. Emergency contact information must be kept current.

4.3 Incident Reporting
All accidents, injuries, and security incidents must be reported within 24 hours. Incident reports should include detailed information about what happened, when, where, and any witnesses. Follow-up investigation may be required.

5. CUSTOMER SERVICE STANDARDS

5.1 Customer First Philosophy
Customer satisfaction is our top priority. All customer interactions should be professional, helpful, and solution-oriented. Customer complaints should be addressed promptly and escalated when necessary.

5.2 Response Time Standards
Phone calls should be answered within 3 rings. Email inquiries should be acknowledged within 4 hours and resolved within 24 hours. Customer service tickets should be updated every 48 hours until resolution.

5.3 Quality Assurance
Customer service interactions may be monitored for quality assurance purposes. Regular training is provided to maintain service standards. Customer feedback is used to continuously improve our service delivery.
